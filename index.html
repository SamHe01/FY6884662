Structural Failures in Coastal Buildings

Under Tsunami Loading: The 2011 Tohoku Earthquake and Tsunami

Abstract

The 2011 Tohoku tsunami caused unprecedented structural failures in coastal areas, offering critical insights into tsunami loading mechanisms and resilience of various building designs. This article examines these failures using Energy Grade Line analysis for flow depth and velocity estimation.

Conclusion

The Tohoku tsunami illustrated the limitations of traditional designs and emphasized the need for tsunami-specific standards. The integration of seismic and tsunami considerations into codes can significantly enhance the resilience of coastal structures.
